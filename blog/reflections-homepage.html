Emotional Intelligence

The first chapter of "Search Inside Yourself" brought up some interesting ideas that I've also seen voiced by psychology writers like gamification proponent Jane McGonigal around the idea of neuroplasticity and our ability to manually reshape our own perceptions. It is a bit terrifying the ways in which the brain can be exploited and re-programmed, even if the intention is to create a happier life for yourself. The most interesting fact the first chapter revealed was the wisdom centre of our brains, the basal ganglia, has no connection with the verbal processing area, which helps explain why sensations and emotions are often very difficult to articulate.

My mindfulness exercise was very relaxing. I am lucky to have a window with a view of some trees so some non-judgmental watching of branches sway proved restful. I will not make the mistake of setting an alarm next time because it is a shock to the system when that goes off after striving to achieve calmness.

First Reflection

Mindset is the biggest upfront message that EDA has focussed on. It is an approach that is tightly aligned to the theories around gameplay style learning where exploration and failure are built in as features to the experience, something that can't be avoided so shouldn't be avoided.

Ultimately you don't always know if you are going to gel with a particular culture or not until you're in it. I like working with people; they brighten up the day and feedback on ideas. This kind of problem solving work is also a huge amount of fun which helps with powering through the small things like existential dread or stuffing up for the hundreth time. My biggest concern is getting into the work in a group situation and not having anything interesting to share or contribute. This never really turns out to be true for anyone as everyone has some unique perspective however rationality and fear are not always entangled so the feeling is hard to dismiss.

Sum-up: this whole adventure will be quite exciting and I look forward to seeing my fingers and a keyboard are capable of producing. Ideally something useful.
